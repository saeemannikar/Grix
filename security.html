Security

Calm security. Clear boundaries.

Grix is built to preserve operational memory from invited Slack channels while keeping customer workspace data separate, controlled, and used only for the product experience.

Core promise Grix only analyzes channels it is invited to.

Security starts with scope. Your team decides where Grix belongs and what context it can help organize.

On this page HTTPS encryption Workspace isolation Access control Operational memory Customer ownership Report an issue

HTTPS encryption

Communication with Grix is encrypted over HTTPS. This helps protect data in transit between your browser, Slack-connected flows, and Grix endpoints.

Workspace isolation

Workspace data is isolated so one customer's workspace content is not exposed to another customer's workspace. Grix treats each workspace as its own trust boundary.

Access control

Grix should be installed and managed by authorized workspace owners, administrators, or approved operators. Customers control which channels Grix is invited to and who is allowed to view workspace outputs.

We recommend inviting Grix only to channels where operational memory is useful and appropriate for your team.

Operational memory processing

Grix processes invited-channel conversations to identify team decisions, tasks, blockers, approvals, reversals, and summaries. The goal is organizational clarity, not individual performance scoring.

Grix does not use customer data to train public AI models and does not sell customer data.

Customer ownership

Your workspace data remains your workspace data. Grix uses it to provide operational memory to your team, subject to the permissions and controls you choose for your workspace.

Workspace owners can request deletion of workspace data at any time.

Report an issue

If you believe you found a security issue or have a security question, contact hello@grix.so. Please include enough detail for us to understand and reproduce the issue.
