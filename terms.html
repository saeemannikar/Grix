Simple terms for a focused product.

Grix exists to help teams preserve operational memory and organizational clarity. These terms keep the boundaries clear for workspace authorization, acceptable use, billing, and deletion requests.

The service

Grix helps Slack workspaces turn invited-channel conversations into operational memory, including decisions, tasks, blockers, approvals, reversals, and summaries. Grix only analyzes channels it is invited to.

The service is provided as a lightweight productivity and alignment layer. It should not be treated as legal, financial, compliance, medical, or employment advice.

Workspace authorization

You are responsible for ensuring that you have the right authority to install Grix, invite it to channels, connect a workspace, and make workspace data available through Grix.

You are also responsible for following your organization's policies and giving any notices or obtaining any consents required for your workspace.

Acceptable use

  • Do not use Grix for unlawful, harmful, deceptive, abusive, or unauthorized purposes.
  • Do not use Grix to evaluate individual employees, create productivity rankings, or build performance-scoring workflows.
  • Do not attempt to bypass Slack permissions, access another workspace's data, or interfere with the service.
  • Do use Grix for operational memory, team alignment, and organizational clarity.

Billing and subscriptions

Grix may offer free trials, free manual analysis, early access plans, or paid subscriptions. Paid plans are billed according to the subscription terms shown when you purchase or agree to a plan.

Subscription features, limits, and pricing may change over time. We will try to communicate material billing changes before they affect active customers.

Service evolution

Grix is evolving. We may add, remove, improve, rename, or change features as the product matures. Some early access features may be experimental and may not always work perfectly.

We work to keep the service reliable, but we do not guarantee that every summary, extraction, or digest will be complete or error-free.

Deletion requests

Workspace owners can request deletion of workspace data by contacting Grix. We may verify workspace ownership or administrative authority before acting on a deletion request.

You can also remove Grix from Slack or revoke access through Slack workspace settings, subject to Slack's controls.
